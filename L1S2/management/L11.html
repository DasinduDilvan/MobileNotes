📖 Introduction

The changing social and business environment gives rise to emerging issues and challenges in management. Managers must continuously acquire knowledge about these new challenges in order to:

  • Modify and adjust themselves to work efficiently
  • Ensure effective and efficient management in dynamic conditions
The changing economic world constantly throws new challenges to managers. Management concepts and practices are actively shaping tomorrow's history.

🌍 1. Globalization of Business

Managers must understand the global context within which their organizations function. Key competencies required:

  • Understanding foreign cultures
  • Ability to penetrate new markets with existing products and services
  • Designing new products and services for new customers
  • Recognizing global competitors
  • Understanding government intervention in major economies of the world
Key Trends in Globalization:
  • Most countries are opening their borders to foreign products as well as foreign producers.
  • Companies from developed countries are entering foreign countries by opening manufacturing facilities there.
  • Management can license or franchise to another firm the right to use its brand name, technology, or product specifications.
  • Global companies are adjusting their organizational structures as per the requirements of each situation.
  • Managers are being trained to think and act globally in approach.
  • Executives from the concerned countries are generally given responsibility for running subsidiaries.

🌱 2. Development of Environmentalism

  • Environmental issues are among the major issues in contemporary management.
  • These issues may not always be a primary concern for enterprises, especially in developing countries.
  • Green movements have spread into different parts of the world.
  • Present managers face the challenge of developing their enterprise creatively, but without harming the environment.

✅ 3. Quality and Productivity

Quality has become one of the most competitive points in business today.

Why Quality is Important
  • High-quality products result in less waste and rework
  • Enhancing quality lowers overall cost
  • Improves productivity across the organization
3 Positive Results of Quality Focus
  • Low return of defects from customers
  • Minimized wastage of resources
  • Reduces the need for an inspector
📋 Total Quality Management (TQM) — Definition

Total Quality Management is the mobilization of the whole organization to achieve quality continuously, economically, and in entirety.

Key Principles of TQM

Beyond Production Quality cannot be improved through the production process alone — it requires improvement in purchasing, marketing, after-sales service, and many other functions.
Coordinated Effort Total quality can be achieved with the co-ordination of various aspects related to purchase, production, sales, etc.
Organization-Wide Participation Total quality control is an effort in which everyone and every function of the organization participates.
Every management has to take care of Total Quality Management — otherwise its products will not find a place in today's competitive world.

⚖️ 4. Ethics and Social Responsibility

Code of Ethics

A formal, written statement of the values and ethical standards that guide a firm's actions.

  • Ethical behavior must start from top management
  • Covers how employees treat suppliers, customers, competitors, etc.
Social Responsibility

The set of obligations an organization has to protect and enhance the societal context in which it functions.

Government's Role in Shaping Social Responsibility:
  • Direct influence: Through regulations
  • Indirect influence: Through taxation policies

👥 5. Workforce Diversity

Definition: Diversity exists in a group or organization when its members differ from one another along one or more important dimensions — e.g., age, gender, ethnicity.
Causes of Increased Diversity
  • Globalization movements
  • Increased awareness
  • Legislation and legal action
  • Changing demographics in the workforce
Diversity as an Asset
  • Brings a broad range of viewpoints
  • Improves problem-solving skills
  • Provides a powerful competitive advantage
Management Shift Required: Managers must shift their philosophy from treating everyone alike to recognizing differences and responding to those differences in ways that ensure employee retention and greater productivity. Organizations must become more accommodating to diverse groups, addressing different lifestyles, family needs, and work styles.

Contingent Workers

Definition: Contingent workers are part-time, temporary, or freelance employees.
  • Some labor experts estimate they make up 13% of the workforce; others suggest as high as 30%.
  • The percentage of contingent workers is increasing every day.
  • Companies have started downsizing their permanent workforce; some displaced employees seek part-time work.

🔄 6. Change

  • Organizational change is a complex phenomenon.
  • Change must be systematic and logical.
  • Managers need to understand the steps of effective change.
  • Managers must know how to counter employee resistance to change.
  • Managements of multinational and transnational companies must study the political and cultural environment of the host country and devise appropriate managerial practices and policies.
Managing Resistance to Change — Key Methods:
Participation
Involve employees in the change process
Education
Train employees about the reason and nature of change
Communication
Keep open, transparent channels throughout
Facilitation
Provide support resources and guidance

Downsizing

  • Downsizing (reduction in force) has been a major trend in recent years.
  • Companies are restructuring organizations and laying off employees who are no longer required.
  • Important: Downsizing does not mean that work has been reduced — in fact, the workload often increases and a smaller workforce must complete it.

💪 7. Empowerment and Team

Definition — Employee Empowerment: Redesigning jobs and letting individual workers and work teams make job-related decisions to improve quality, productivity, and employee commitment.
  • Workers at present are considered more knowledgeable and are expected to complete their work in a better way.
  • Sometimes, workers are considered to perform better than even their managers.
  • Many organizations have achieved better results by empowering employees and allowing them to plan and execute their work.
  • Human resource theorists have criticized over-specialization of employees as it stifles their capabilities.
Benefits of Empowerment: Improved quality, higher productivity, and stronger employee commitment — achieved by redesigning jobs and granting teams decision-making authority.

🏪 8. Shift Towards a Service Economy

  • The service sector of the economy has become increasingly important.
  • There is a significant difference between managing manufacturing organizations and service organizations — managers must adapt their approach accordingly.
