What is an Environment?

"The term environment is used to describe, in the aggregate, all the external and internal forces, influences, and conditions, which affect the life, nature, behaviour, and the growth, development, and maturity of living organisms."
‚Äî Douglas and Holland
Simple Definition:
The surroundings or conditions in which a person, animal, or plant lives or operates.

Business Environment

"The business environment is the aggregate of all the conditions, events, and influences that surround and affect it".
‚Äî Keith Davis
Business environment refers to those aspects of the surroundings of a business enterprise that affect or influence its operations and determine its effectiveness.
Key Concept:
The business environment is the set of forces surrounding an organization. A key element in effective management is determining the ideal alignment between the organization and its environment, and working to achieve and maintain that alignment.

External Environment

"The external environment is everything outside an organization's boundaries that might affect it"
‚Äî Griffin, 2016
There are two separate external environments:
General Environment The set of broad dimensions and forces in an organization's surroundings that create its overall context.

Includes: Economic, Technological, Sociocultural, Political & Legal, and International dimensions
Task Environment Specific organizations or groups that affect the organization.

Includes: Competitors, Customers, Suppliers, Regulators, and Strategic Allies

General Environment - Five Dimensions

Economic Dimension The overall health of the economic system in which the organization operates

Examples: Inflation, interest rates, unemployment
Technological Dimension Methods available for converting resources into products and services

Examples: Computer-assisted systems, AI, Big data
Sociocultural Dimension Customs, values, and demographic characteristics of the society

Examples: Consumer taste, preferences, behaviour
Political & Legal Dimension The government regulation of business and the general relationship between business and government

Examples: Government regulation of business, laws, policies
International Dimension The extent to which an organization is involved in or affected by business in other countries

Examples: An organization is affected by businesses in other countries

PESTLE Analysis

A comprehensive framework for analyzing the General Environment
P - Political
How government actions and policies influence the market and your organisation. Examples: Election results, legislation changes, trade agreements.
E - Economic
Broader economy's health and its impact on your organisation. This includes: Inflation rates, economic growth, exchange rates.
S - Social
Cultural and demographic aspects of the external environment. This looks at: Population growth, age distribution, cultural trends, and lifestyle changes.
T - Technological
Incorporates innovation and technological changes that could affect your market position or operations. Examples: Advancements in digital technology.
L - Legal
Involves the regulatory environment in which you operate. Compliance with laws and regulations at local, national, and international levels.
E - Environmental
Ecological and environmental aspects that could impact your operations or market. This includes: Climate change and sustainable practices.

Task Environment

Competitors An organization that competes with other organizations for resources
Customers Individuals who pay money to acquire an organization's products and services
Suppliers An organization that provides resources for other organizations
Strategic Allies An organization working together with one or more other organizations in a joint venture or similar arrangements
Regulators A unit that has the potential to control, legislate, or otherwise influence the organization's policies and practices

Activity - Small Group Discussion

Title: "Impact of PESTLE Factors on a Local Business"
Instructions:
  1. Select a local company or industry
  2. Analyze each PESTLE factor and note real-world examples for each
  3. Discuss how these factors create opportunities or threats
  4. Prepare to present your findings in 2 minutes briefly

Key Takeaways

Remember:
  • Business environment includes all external and internal forces affecting an organization
  • Two main environments: Internal (within organization) and External (outside organization)
  • External environment has two levels: General Environment (PESTLE) and Task Environment (specific stakeholders)
  • PESTLE Analysis: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental
  • Task Environment includes: Competitors, Customers, Suppliers, Strategic Allies, Regulators
  • Porter's Five Forces: New entrants, Competitive rivalry, Substitutes, Buyer power, Supplier power
  • Organizations adapt through: Information management, Strategic response, Mergers/alliances, Design flexibility
  • Business environment is complex, dynamic, interdependent, uncertain, and relative
  • Studying business environment helps identify opportunities and threats
