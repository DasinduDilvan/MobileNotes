🔧 What is Organizing?

Definition: "Organizing is the process of identifying and grouping the work to be performed, defining and delegating responsibility and authority, and establishing relationships for the purpose of enabling people to work most efficiently."

Organizing involves:

  • Deciding how best to group organizational activities and resources.
  • A systematic arrangement of the activities.
  • Managers can design their organizations in different ways.
Organization Structure — The set of elements that can be used to configure an organization. It is a key element in organizational design.

⚙️ Fundamental Concepts of Organizing

Differentiation An organization is composed of units that work on specialized tasks using different work methods and requiring employees with unique competencies.
Integration The various units must be put back together so that work is coordinated across the organization.

🔍 Factors Affecting Organizational Structure

The following four factors determine the design of organizational structure:

🌍 Organizational EnvironmentThe external context in which the organization operates.
💻 TechnologyThe tools, systems, and processes used in operations.
♟️ StrategyThe long-term plans and goals of the organization.
👥 Human ResourcesThe skills, needs, and characteristics of employees.

🔄 Process of Organizing

1. Identification of Activities
2. Grouping of Activities
3. Delegation of Authority
4. Assignment of Duties
Identification of Activities Determine the tasks that must be performed to achieve the established objectives.
Grouping of Activities Various activities are grouped into departments or divisions according to similarity and common purpose.
Delegation of Authority Every individual is given the authority required to carry out the responsibility assigned to them.
Assignment of Duties Groups of activities or departments are allotted to different positions. Every position is occupied by an individual best suited for it.
Steps in Organizing also include:
  • Identification and classification of required activities.
  • Grouping of activities necessary to attain objectives.
  • Assignment of each grouping to a manager with necessary authority to supervise it.
  • Provision for co-ordination horizontally and vertically in the organizational structure.

⭐ Importance of Organizing

  • Facilitates Administration: Achieves objectives by providing a framework of coordination and control.
  • Encourages Growth and Diversification: Enables organizations to grow and expand to giant sizes.
  • Optimum Use of New Technology: Made through a sound structure manned with competent employees.
  • Stimulates Innovation & Creativity: Stimulates creative thinking and initiative on the part of employees.
  • Encourages Good Human Relations: Assignment of the right jobs to the right person improves job satisfaction and interpersonal relations.
  • Ensures Continuity of Enterprise: Provides scope for training and development of future management.
  • Coordination: Facilitates order and cohesiveness in the enterprise.

🏛️ Formal & Informal Organization Structures

Formal Organization The intentional structure of roles in a formally organized enterprise.
Informal Organization A network of personal and social relations not established/required by the formal organization but arising spontaneously as people associate with one another.

Characteristic Features of Formal Organization

  • Structure is laid down by top management to achieve organizational goals.
  • Defines relationships among people working in the organization.
  • Enables people to work together to accomplish common objectives.
  • Concentrates on the jobs to be performed, not the individuals who perform them.
  • Individuals are fitted into jobs and work as per managerial decisions.
  • Bound by rules, regulations, and procedures.
  • Position, authority, responsibility, and accountability of each level are clearly defined.
  • Based on the division of labor and specialization to achieve efficiency.
  • Coordination proceeds according to the prescribed pattern.

Advantages of Formal Organization

  • Makes everybody responsible for a given task (concentrates on jobs to be performed).
  • Bound by rules and regulations — ensures law and order in the organization.
  • Enables people to work together to accomplish common objectives of the enterprise.

Characteristic Features of Informal Organization

  • Not established by any formal authority; unplanned and arises spontaneously.
  • Reflects human relationships — arises from personal and social relations among people.
  • Natural process; not based on rules, regulations, and procedures.
  • Inter-relations among people cannot be shown in an organization chart.
  • People cut across formal channels of communication and communicate directly.
  • Membership is voluntary; arises spontaneously, not by conscious efforts.
  • Based on common tastes, problems, language, religion, culture, etc. Influenced by personal attitudes, emotions, whims, likes and dislikes.

Benefits of Informal Organization

  • Blends with the formal organization to make it more effective.
  • Achieves things that cannot be achieved through formal organization alone.
  • Makes managers plan and act more carefully.
  • Best means of fast employee communication.
  • Has a powerful influence on productivity and job satisfaction.
  • Helps group members attain specific personal objectives.

📏 Span of Control

Definition: Refers to the number of subordinates who report directly to an executive or supervisor.
  • Differences in the span of control have direct implications on the shape of the organization.
  • Organizations must find the optimal span of control to be effective.
  • Narrow enough to permit managers to maintain control over subordinates.
  • Wide enough so that the possibility of micromanaging is minimized.
  • Depends on complexity of the organization, nature of relationships between supervisors and subordinates, and the need for optimum organizational output.

Dimensions of Span of Control

Horizontal The number of direct subordinates that managers actually supervise/manage.
Vertical The number of levels that managers (in)directly supervise/manage.

Narrow Span — Advantages & Disadvantages

✅ Advantages
  • Close supervision
  • Close control
  • Fast communication between subordinates and superiors
❌ Disadvantages
  • Superiors tend to get too involved in subordinates' work
  • Many levels of management
  • High costs due to many levels
  • Excessive distance between lowest and top level

Wide Span — Advantages & Disadvantages

✅ Advantages
  • Superiors are forced to delegate
  • Clear policies must be made
  • Subordinates must be carefully selected
❌ Disadvantages
  • Tendency of overloaded superiors to become decision bottlenecks
  • Danger of superior's loss of control
  • Requires exceptional quality of managers
Tall vs. Flat Organizations: A tall organization has many hierarchical levels (narrow span), while a flat organization has few levels (wide span). Tall structures offer close control; flat structures offer faster communication and more autonomy.

Factors Influencing Span of Control

  • Resources available
  • Competence of the manager
  • Nature of work
  • Assistance available to managers
  • Competence of subordinates
  • Organizational levels
  • Plans, policies, and regulations

🛠️ Designing Jobs

Job design involves the determination of an individual's work-related responsibilities. It specifies:

  • What mechanics are to be operated and how they are to be operated.
  • What the expected performance standards are.
  • Areas of decision-making responsibility.
  • Goals and expectations.
  • Appropriate indicators of success.

🎯 Job Specialization

Definition: The degree to which the overall task of the organization is broken down and divided into smaller component parts.
✅ Benefits
  • Workers become talented in their tasks.
  • Less transfer time between tasks.
  • Easier to develop specialized equipment to assist with the job.
  • Easier to replace positions at a lower cost.
❌ Limitations
  • Jobs may become boring and employees dissatisfied.
  • Absenteeism will rise and quality of work may suffer.
  • Benefits of specialization do not always occur.

Alternatives to Job Specialization

Alternative Description
Job Rotation Systematically moving employees from one job to another.
Job Enlargement Giving the employee more tasks to perform.
Job Enrichment Increasing both the number of tasks the worker does and the control the worker has over the job.
Work Team Allows an entire group to design the work system it will use to perform an interrelated set of tasks.

🏗️ Departmentalization

Definition: The process of grouping jobs according to some logical arrangement.

Common Bases for Departmentalization

Functional The grouping of activities by the functions performed (e.g., HR, Finance, Marketing).
Product The grouping of activities by product produced.
Customer The grouping of activities by common customers served.
Location / Geographic The grouping of activities by territory or region.

📊 Reporting Relationships

Reporting relationships among positions are reflected by:

Chain of Command A clear and distinct line of authority among the positions in an organization.
Span of Management The number of people who report to a particular manager.

⚖️ Distributing Authority

Authority — Power that has been legitimized by the organization. It involves the sub-division and sub-allocation of power to subordinates.

When distributing authority, managers must address two key issues: Delegation and Decentralization.

Delegation

Definition: The process by which a manager assigns a portion of his or her total workload to others.

Steps in the Delegation Process:

1. Assigning Responsibility
2. Granting Authority
3. Creating Accountability

Elements of Delegation:

Authority The power or right given to a manager/employee to make decisions, give orders, and use resources. Flows Top Down.
Responsibility The duty or obligation assigned to an individual to perform a task. Flows Bottom Up.
Accountability The obligation of a subordinate to report back on task performance and results. Ensures authority and responsibility are exercised properly.

Benefits of Delegation:

  • Helps managers by doing part of their work.
  • Subordinates may sometimes have more experience in specific areas.
  • Improves managerial skills by participating in decision-making and problem-solving.

Limitations of Delegation:

  • Managers reluctant to delegate — due to disorganization, worry that subordinates do well and become a threat, or lack of trust.
  • Subordinates avoid accepting delegation — due to risk aversion, fear of responsibility, or perceiving no reward.

Advantages of Delegation:

Sharing of workload
Quick decision-making
Motivation
Training
Better performance
Better relations

Decentralization vs. Centralization

Decentralization The process of systematically delegating power and authority throughout the organization to middle and lower-level managers.
Centralization The process of systematically retaining power and authority in the hands of higher-level managers.

🌐 Global Organizing

Definition: Global organizing refers to structuring and coordinating business activities across multiple countries to achieve efficiency, competitiveness, and adaptation to local markets.

It involves balancing two forces:

Global Integration Achieving efficiency and consistency across all international operations.
Local Responsiveness Adapting to culture, laws, and customer needs in each specific market.

Team-Based OrganizationWork is structured around collaborative teams rather than traditional hierarchy.

Virtual OrganizationAn organization that exists primarily through digital connections without a central physical location.
