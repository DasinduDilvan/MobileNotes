What is Management?

Management Formula

Using Resources ‚Üí Set of Activities ‚Üí Achieving Goals
Resource-based Perspective: Describes how managers combine and coordinate the various kinds of resources.
Ricky W. Griffin Definition:
"A set of activities (including planning and decision making, organizing, leading, and controlling) directed at an organization's resources (human, financial, physical, and information), with the aim of achieving organizational goals in an efficient and effective manner."
Key Definitions:
  • General Definition: Management in all business and organizational activities is the act of getting people together to accomplish desired goals and objectives using available resources efficiently and effectively
  • Mary Parker Follett: "Management is the art of getting things done through others"
  • F W Taylor: "Management is knowing exactly what you want to do and then seeing that they do it the best and cheapest way"

Who is a Manager?

Manager's Role

A Person ‚Üí Activities ‚Üí Resources ‚Üí Goals
Definition 1: A manager is someone whose primary responsibility is to carry out the management process.
Definition 2: A manager is a person who plans and makes decisions, organizes, leads, and controls human, financial, physical, and information resources.
Definition 3: Someone who coordinates and oversees the work of other people so that organizational goals can be accomplished.

Management Process - The Five Functions

Management Functions Flow

Planning ‚Üí Organizing ‚Üí Staffing ‚Üí Directing ‚Üí Controlling

1. Planning

First function performed by every Manager
  • Planning refers to "deciding in advance what to do, how to do, when to do, and who is going to do it"
  • Planning bridges the gap between where we stand today and where we want to reach
  • Every manager starts with deciding in advance the objectives of an enterprise and how to accomplish these objectives
  • Planning is the base of all other functions of management
  • "Thinking before action"

2. Organizing

  • After setting up plans, next function is to organize the activities and establish an organizational structure to execute the plan
  • Setting up organizational structure means:
    • Deciding the framework of working
    • How many units, sub-units, or departments are needed
    • How many posts or designations are needed in each department
    • How to distribute authority and responsibility among different people
  • Once these decisions are taken, an organizational structure gets set up

3. Staffing

Placement of the right man on the right job at the right time
  • Recruiting, selecting, appointing the employees
  • Assigning them duties and responsibilities
  • Maintaining fair relations and taking care of grievances of employees
  • Training and developing the employees
  • Deciding their remuneration, promotion, increment, etc.
  • Evaluating the performance
  • Maintaining personal records of employees

4. Directing

Guiding the subordinates towards best attainment of common objectives
  • Once employees are appointed, there is need to instruct them and get the work done
  • Directing refers to giving instructions to employees by:
    • Motivating them
    • Supervising their activities
    • Communicating with them
  • Managers act as leaders and guide them to right direction
  • Directing function includes: supervising, motivating, communicating, and leadership

5. Controlling

Last function of managers
  • Managers try to match actual performance with planned performance
  • If there is no match, managers:
    • Find out the reasons of deviation
    • Suggest corrective measures to come on path of plan
  • Controlling refers to all performance measurements and follow-up actions that keep actual performance on the path of plan
  • Ensures actions of people working throughout the enterprise are according to plan

Top Managers

  • A small group of executives
  • Manage the overall organization
  • Establish the organization's goals, overall strategy, and operating policies
  • Officially represent the organization to the external environment
  • Make key decisions on investments, marketing, research and development
  • Work long hours
  • Spend more time in meetings and discussions
Examples: President, Vice President, Chief Executive Officer (CEO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Chief Operating Officer (COO)
Main Functions of Top Managers:
  • Determining the objectives of enterprise
  • Framing of plans and policies
  • Organizing activities to be performed by persons working at middle level
  • Assembling all the resources such as finance, fixed assets
  • Responsible for welfare and survival of the organization
  • Affair with outside world such as meeting government officials

Middle Managers

  • A large group of managers
  • Responsible for implementing the policies and plans developed by top managers
  • Supervise and coordinate the activities of lower-level managers
Examples: Plant manager, operations manager, division head, department heads (purchase, sales, finance, marketing), executive officer, plant superintendent
Main Functions of Middle Managers:
  • Interpretation of policies made by top level management
  • Organizing the activities of their department
  • Finding out, recruiting, selecting and appointing the required employees
  • Motivating the persons to perform their best ability
  • Controlling and instructing the employees
  • Cooperating with other departments for smooth functioning
  • Implementing the plans formed by top level

First-Line (Lower) Managers

  • Supervise and coordinate the activities of operating employees
  • Spend more time for supervising subordinates
Examples: Supervisor, Coordinator, Office Manager, Superintendent, Foreman, Sub-department Executives, Clerk
Main Functions of Lower Managers:
  • Representing problems of workers
  • Maintaining good working conditions and developing healthy relations between superior and subordinate
  • Looking after safety of workers
  • Helping the middle level management in recruiting, selecting and appointing the workers
  • Communicating with workers and welcoming their suggestions
  • Maintaining precise standard of quality
  • Responsible for boosting the morale of the workers

Kinds of Managers by Area

Marketing Managers Work in areas related to the marketing function

Examples: Product development and promotion, sales and distribution
Financial Managers Deal with organization's financial resources

Examples: Accounting, cash management, investments
Operations Managers Create and manage systems that create an organization's products and services

Examples: Production control, quality control, inventory control
Human Resources Managers Responsible for hiring and developing employees

Examples: HR planning, selection and recruiting, training, HR development
Administrative Managers Responsible for all managerial activities of an organization

Have basic familiarity with all functional areas
Other Kinds of Managers Appointed according to organizational requirements

Examples: Client Relations Manager, R&D Manager, Credit Manager
